Step-by-Step Strategy

Step 1: Product Selection & Inventory Sourcing

Identify products with US appeal and scale potential. Focus on ODOP products or other MSME-produced goods with export viability.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistake to Avoid

Launching multiple SKUs without testing demand can result in inventory issues and lost capital.

Step 2: Choose Sales Channel

Decide between marketplace listings (Amazon US, eBay US) and your own brand site. Marketplaces provide instant reach and credibility; brand sites offer higher margins but require marketing investment.

"Selling overseas requires both strategy and patience ‚Äî start small, test, then scale."

Step 3: Compliance & Export Logistics

Ensure HS code classification, licensing, and GST compliance. Understand US import standards and choose fulfillment methods wisely (FBA, 3PL, or direct shipping).

‚úÖ Pro Tip

Partner with freight forwarders familiar with US e-commerce regulations to minimize delays and hidden costs.

Tools and Resources

  • Amazon Global Selling: Provides access to US marketplaces, payment processing, and fulfillment options.
  • IndiaMART/Export.gov: Useful for finding buyers and understanding export requirements.
  • Freight Forwarders/3PLs: Reduce shipping complications and costs.

Practical Implementation

30-Day Action Plan

  • Week 1: Identify 1-2 SKUs suitable for export; calculate landed cost and potential US price.
  • Week 2: Open US marketplace seller account and analyze competitor listings.
  • Week 3: Set up logistics and fulfill pilot shipment. Prepare US-compliant product listings.
  • Week 4: Launch pilot batch; monitor metrics like conversion, shipping times, and returns.

Common Challenges and Solutions

  • Challenge 1: High shipping costs
    Solution: Negotiate with logistics partners, use FBA, focus on light-weight, high-margin SKUs.
  • Challenge 2: Returns and customer dissatisfaction
    Solution: Make listings crystal-clear, use accurate US measurements, build return plan.
  • Challenge 3: Regulatory compliance changes
    Solution: Keep updated on ODOP, MSME, and US import policies to avoid penalties.
